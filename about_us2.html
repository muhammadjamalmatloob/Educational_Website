ABOUT US

OUR HISTORY


Founded in 1999, the Pakistan Institute of Science and Technology (PIST) has become a distinguished center for STEM education and research, located in the vibrant city of Lahore. Established with a vision to advance knowledge and innovation, PIST was created to address Pakistan's growing demand for high-quality education in Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics.

Over the years, PIST has expanded its academic offerings and research initiatives, fostering a dynamic learning environment that equips students with the skills and knowledge to excel in a rapidly evolving global landscape. Our institution has grown from a small cohort of passionate students and faculty into a thriving community dedicated to scientific discovery, technological advancement, and societal impact.

Today, PIST is recognized for its commitment to academic excellence and its contributions to groundbreaking research in engineering and technology. We continue to empower future leaders, innovators, and change-makers who will shape the world.

NOTABLE ALUMNI

Dr. Zain Ali - Cybersecurity Expert and Tech Entrepreneur

Dr. Zain Ali, an accomplished alumnus of the Pakistan Institute of Science and Technology’s Computer Science program, graduated in 2012 and has since become a prominent figure in cybersecurity. Driven by a mission to enhance digital security, Dr. Ali founded SecureWave Technologies, a company specializing in advanced cybersecurity solutions for businesses and government agencies in Pakistan. His groundbreaking work in threat detection and secure data management has protected countless organizations from cyberattacks, earning him the National Award for Cyber Innovation. As an advocate for cybersecurity education, Dr. Ali frequently returns to PIST to conduct workshops, inspiring the next generation of tech professionals to safeguard the digital future. His contributions have positioned him as a leading voice in Pakistan’s tech community, embodying the innovation and dedication encouraged at PIST.

Engr. Ahmed Raza - Renewable Energy Entrepreneur

Engr. Ahmed Raza, a proud graduate of the Pakistan Institute of Science and Technology's Electrical Engineering program in 2011, has become a leading figure in renewable energy innovation across Pakistan. With a vision to address the country's energy crisis, Ahmed founded EcoTech Solutions, a startup focused on developing affordable solar energy solutions for rural communities. Under his leadership, EcoTech has installed over 500 solar grids, bringing sustainable power to remote areas and transforming thousands of lives. Ahmed’s work has earned him international accolades, including the Young Leaders in Sustainable Energy award. His commitment to sustainable development and passion for social impact make him a role model for aspiring engineers and a cherished member of the PIST community.

Dr. Bilal Khan - Automotive Engineer and Innovator

Dr. Bilal Khan, a graduate of the Pakistan Institute of Science and Technology’s Mechanical Engineering program in 2005, has made his mark as a visionary in the automotive industry. Known for his expertise in electric vehicle technology, Dr. Khan played a crucial role in designing Pakistan’s first locally manufactured electric vehicle, aimed at promoting eco-friendly transportation. After completing advanced research abroad, he returned to Pakistan and joined hands with local automakers to develop affordable, sustainable vehicles tailored for the country’s roads. His work has not only advanced Pakistan’s automotive sector but has also contributed significantly to reducing carbon emissions. Recognized globally, Dr. Khan was awarded the Automotive Innovators Award, making him a source of inspiration for future engineers at PIST and beyond.

Prof. Saad Farooq - Renowned Robotics Expert and Educator

Prof. Saad Farooq, an esteemed alumnus of the Pakistan Institute of Science and Technology, graduated in 2010 with a degree in Mechanical Engineering. Today, he is celebrated for his pioneering work in robotics and automation. After earning his doctorate from a prestigious university abroad, Prof. Farooq returned to Pakistan to lead innovative research in robotics at a top engineering university. He has developed cutting-edge robotic systems that assist in industries ranging from manufacturing to agriculture, helping local businesses increase efficiency and reduce costs. His research has garnered international recognition, and he was honored with the Innovative Educators in Robotics award. Prof. Farooq’s commitment to advancing robotics in Pakistan has made him a respected leader in the field, inspiring countless PIST students to pursue careers in technology and automation.

Engr. Omar Sheikh - Smart Grid Specialist and Energy Innovator

Engr. Omar Sheikh, a distinguished graduate of the Pakistan Institute of Science and Technology’s Electrical Engineering program in 2007, has established himself as a leader in the field of smart grid technology. With a passion for sustainable energy solutions, Omar has spearheaded projects to modernize Pakistan’s aging power grid, integrating smart technologies that enhance energy efficiency and reliability. As the founder of GreenGrid Technologies, he has introduced smart meters and automated systems to reduce energy losses and manage demand effectively, benefiting both urban and rural communities. His contributions have earned him the Energy Innovators Award and recognition as one of the region’s top experts in renewable and smart grid technology. Omar’s work continues to inspire future engineers at PIST, as he frequently returns to campus to share his insights and mentor students eager to make a difference in Pakistan’s energy landscape.

Engr. Faisal Hussain - Infrastructure Visionary and Sustainability Advocate

Engr. Faisal Hussain, a 2006 graduate of the Pakistan Institute of Science and Technology’s Civil Engineering program, has made remarkable strides in sustainable infrastructure development. As a leading civil engineer and the founder of EcoBuild Pakistan, Faisal has focused on designing resilient, eco-friendly infrastructure across the country. His projects include the construction of flood-resistant housing in rural areas and the implementation of green building practices in urban centers, prioritizing materials and methods that reduce environmental impact. Recognized with the Sustainable Infrastructure Pioneer Award, Faisal’s work has been transformative for Pakistan’s construction industry. His commitment to innovative and sustainable solutions has made him a role model for PIST students and a key figure in Pakistan’s drive towards sustainable development.
